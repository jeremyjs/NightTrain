INSTRUCTORS

Our instructors this year are Mike Legett & Shawn Hershey

Mike Legett

Mike is a passionate aficionado of jazz and blues dances, known all over the world for her friendliness, teaching skills, beautiful following, and expressive dancing.  She brings both vibrant energy and technical precision to the classroom, and strives to make every social dance the best experience possible.  Mike’s teaching history is extensive; having taught at a range of events from residencies to international workshops to private trainings, she brings preparation and flexibility to every class.  A typical class could involve students morphing into robot penguins or seaweed, standing on one foot with their eyes closed, or analyzing chord changes- all in the pursuit of better dancing.  Regardless of the material, her classes always include a mix of abstract and concrete topics, plenty of levity, and challenging homework to help students improve long after class has ended.

Shawn Hershey

Shawn Hershey came from an extensive music background, studying classical trumpet performance at the Eastman School of Music, so when he started lindy hopping in 1999 with the Lindybaby studio, he quickly got hooked! He has studied extensively at camps and workshops with nationally renowned instructors.  In 2002, he started teaching swing and lindy hop in Boston around with various partners.  Shawn has also taught and performed lindy hop internationally with his sister, Betina Hershey. Since Shawn and Amanda joined forces in 2005, they have collectively won or placed in many national blues competitions, and have taught and performed together at workshops and events around North America, including Austin Blues Party, Down Home Blues, the Fusion Exchange, and BluesSHOUT! Their style of blending lindy hop, ballroom, blues, and Argentine tango into their dancing is both beautiful and powerful, and earns them praise wherever they go. Shawn also teaches locally and nationally with Julie Brown and has taught with Mike Legett, Heidi Fite, Joy Arico, and Kathy Warwick. Currently, Shawn teaches with a collective of Boston dance instructors at New School Swing in Charlestown, MA and Blues Union in Somerville, MA.  He also runs two successful swing and blues bands, The Fried Bananas and the Shawn Hershey Quartet.

CLASS DESCRIPTIONS

Saturday

  • Magic
    One of the secrets to becoming a great partner dancer is ______.  In this class we'll work on quality of_____ in prepa;ration for ______. _______ will change your life forever because it is the secret to excellent blues dancing!
  • Comfy Close Embrace
    How to make your close embrace feel The Best!  When done right, close embrace is awesome.  We'll incorporate the solo movement from the previous class, learn a few moves, and focus on the magic behind making close embrace awesome and musical.
  • Moves that go Weeeee!
    Playing with the flow of momentum is soooo fun.  We'll work on the techniques involved in following and leading spinning and momentum based blues moves.
  • Putting the Blues in Blues Dancing
    In this class we'll work on the fundamentals of jukin', including simple and complex solo body movement, and how to connect that to your partner.
  • Invention!
    - Coming up with new things can be a very fun and exciting process.  By the end of this class, everyone will have produced something never seen since the beginnings of the universe!

Sunday

  • Ruthithms
    In this class we'll get you dancing rhythms that are SO FUN!  You'll be like another instrument in the mix, inspiring your partner, and yourself.
  • Drag Blues
    Bring a helmet!  No, no, really, it will be fine.  Drag blues involves some close embrace flash, a little bit of weight sharing, and a whole lot of fun.  Learn it!
  • All That Jazzzz
    Get all up inside the music in ways that are the funnest!  We'll work on creating giant musical arcs, big movements, small movements, motifs, and more.
